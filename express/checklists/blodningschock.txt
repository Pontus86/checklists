Title/1. Mechanical Hemostatic Measures?
Text/
• Wound:  pressure dressing, tourniquet
• Pelvic ring fracture: pelvic binder
• Severe intrathoracic: acute thoracotomy
• Severe pelvic/abdominal: proximal external aortic compression (knee)
• Severe vaginal bleed: proximal external aortic compression (knee), bimanual uterus, compression, balloon tamponade of uterus
• Femur fracture: reduction and traction splint



Title/2. Initial Blood Tests
Text/
• pH, BE, HCO3-, lactate, Hb, electrolytes; thrombocytes, INR, aPTT, fibrinogen, blood type



Title/3. Target Blood Pressure
Text/
• SBP 80-90 mm Hg
• SBP > 100 mm Hg if traumatic brain injury or spinal cord injury



Title/4. Crystalloids?
Text/
Indication: until pRBCs are available, max 1 liter

• Ringer’s acetate
• 0.9% NaCl if severe head trauma



Title/5. pRBC
Text/
Indication: suspected massive haemorrhage

• O neg blood for fertile women
• O neg or pos blood for others
• 1-2 units WARM via rapid infuser
• Activate major haemorrhage protocol if > 2 pRBC



Title/6. Drugs
Text/
• Cyklokapron 2 g (30 mg/kg) IV över 10 min om trauma < 3h or icke-GI blödning
• Fibrinogen (Riastap) 2 g (30 mg/kg) IV om massiv blödning
• Kalcium glukonat 100 mg/ml 10 ml om Ca < 1,15 (0,3 ml/kg)

• Vid Waran: Ocplex 2000 IE IV (80 ml) + Konakion 10 mg IV
• Vid Xarelto/Eliquis: Ocplex 2000 IE IV (80 ml)
• Vid Pradaxa: Ocplex 2000 IE IV (80 ml) eller Idarucizumab 5 g IV över 5 min
• Vid Trombyl/NSAID: Desmopressin 15 ug/ml 1 ml (50 kg)  - 2 ml (100 kg) SC
• Vid ofraktionerad heparin/LMWH: Protamine sulfate 50 mg

• If upper GI bleed:  Nexium 80 mg IV
• If upper GI bleed + liver disease:  Terlipressin 2 mg IV + Cefotaxime 1 g IV
• If post-partum hemorrhage:  Oxytocin (Syntocinon) 8.3 ug/ml 1 ml (5 IE) IV over 1 min



Title/7. Tubes?
Text/
• Foley catheter to follow hourly diuresis
• Chest tube for suspected hemothorax. > 1 L blood loss suggests need for surgery
• Nasogastric tube may confirm upper GI-bleed but poor sensitivity
• Endotracheal intubation to secure the airway (caution hypotension)



Title/8. Additional Measures?
Text/
• Prevent/correct hypothermia
• Antibiotics, e.g. Cloxacillin (Ekvacillin®) 2 g IV (≤ 20 kg:  50 mg/kg) for open extremity fractures
• Tetanus prophylaxis for open wounds (diTeBooster® 0.5 ml IM)



Title/9. Further Management
Text/
• Massive Hemorrhage Protocol:  administer thrombocytes when available (not through rapid infuser).  Administer FFP, target 2:1:1 pRCB:FFP:Thrombocytes over coming hours.
• Laparotomy/thoracotomy for on-going intraabdominal/intrathoracic hemorrhage
• Angiography and embolization/EVAR for pelvic bleeding/ruptured AAA
• Endoscopy for patients with significant upper or lower gastrointestinal bleeding
• CT if unknown bleeding source AND patient stabilized after initial resuscitation
