Title/1. Hypertensive Myocardial Ischemia
Text/
• IV vasodilator (e.g. Nitroglycerin)

• If insufficient effect, add IV beta-blockers (Labetalol, Esmolol, Metoprolol)



Title/2. Hypertensive Pulmonary Edema
Text/
• IV vasodilator (Nitroglycerin, Nitroprusside)

• Add loop diuretics (Furosemide) if volume overloaded (e.g. associated renal failure)

• See Cardiogenic Pulmonary Edema



Title/3. Aortic Dissection
Text/
• See Aortic Dissection



Title/4. Hypertensive Emergency During Pregnancy
Text/
• Treat if SBP > 160 mm Hg or DBP > 110 mm Hg.  Labetalol IV, target SBT 140-160 mm Hg and DBP 90-110 mm Hg, max MAP reduction 25% over the first two hours

• Magnesium sulfate 5 g (Addex®-Magnesium 1 mmol/ml 20 ml) IV over 5 minutes loading dose 



Title/5. Sympathomimetic Crisis / Cocaine-Amphetamine Toxicity
Text/
• If agitated: benzodiazepines IV (e.g. Diazepam 5 mg IV, repeat as needed)

• Nitroprusside or phentolamine IV.  Avoid beta-blockers (which may lead to unopposed alpha receptor stimulation and ischemia)



Title/6. Hypertensive Neurological Emergencies
Text/
• Treatment depends on CT-head findings and whether patient is candidate for thrombolysis

• The diagnosis of hypertensive encephalopathy is usually made retrospectively



Title/Nitroprusside Dosage
Text/
• Infusion 0.3 μg/kg/min, increase up to 10 μg/kg/min



Title/Nitroglycerin Dosage
Text/
• Bolus 0.4 mg SL every 60 seconds; or 2 mg bolus IV q3min

• Infusion 10–20 μg/min, titrade rapidly up to 200 μg/min



Title/Labetalol Dosage
Text/
• Bolus 20 mg IV initially then 20-80 mg q10 min to total dose 300 mg

• Infusion 0.5 to 2 mg/min



Title/Esmolol Dosage
Text/
• Bolus	0.5 mg/kg over 1 min

• Infusion 50 μg/kg/min, titrade up to 300 μg/kg/min
