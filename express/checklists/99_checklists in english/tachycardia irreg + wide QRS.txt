Title/1. ABCDE
Text/
• B: respiratory insufficiency? Pulmonary oedema?
• C: blood pressure? Shock?
• D: level of consciousness?
• E: fever? Leg swelling?

Consider
• Oxygen 10 L/min
• Large PVC


Title/2. Emergent Cardioversion?
Text/
Indication: severe symptoms (resp insufficiency, shock) due to the tachycardia

Contraindication: tachycardia and instability secondary to another condition (e.g. sepsis, pulmonary embolism) 

• Sedation with Propofol 0.5 mg/ml or Ketamine 1 mg/kg IV over 1 min

• SYNCHRONIZE

• 200 J (2 J/kg)

• If refractory:  Amiodarone 300 mg in glucose 50 mg/ml 100 ml IV over 10 min then reattempt cardioversion


Title/3. EKG
Text/
Talar för VT:
• Negative or positive concordance
• AV dissociation, capture beat, fusion beat
• QRS > 160 msec
• Extreme axis (negative in I and II)
• RS (onset R to nadir S) in precordial lead > 100 msec



Title/4. Lewis Lead?  Esophageal EKG?
Text/
• Right arm electrode on 2nd ICS right sternal border
• Left arm electrode on 4th ICS right sternal border
• Increase amplitude to 20 mm/mV
• Record EKG, look for the P wave in lead I

Alternative:  esophageal EKG



Title/5. Hyperkalemia?  Hypokalemia?
Text/
• Hyperkalemia can cause wide QRS complexes.  Treatment with antiarrhythmics can be fatal.

• Hypokalemia and suspected VT:  Magnesium Sulphate 8 mmmol (Addex-Magnesium 1 mmol/ml 8 ml) IV over 2 min



Title/6. Na-Channel blockade?
Text/
• Poisoning with Na-channel blockers (e.g. tricyclics) can cause wide QRS complexes
• Treatment with antiarrhythmics can be fatal



Title/7. Ventricular Tachycardia?
Text/
Two options:
• Amiodarone 300 mg in Glucose 50 mg/ml IV over 30 min

• Cardioversion (see point 2)
