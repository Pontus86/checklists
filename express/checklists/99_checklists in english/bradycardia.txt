Title/1. Cardiac Arrest?
Text/
• CPR

• Adrenalin 0.1 mg/ml 10 ml (10 µg/kg) IV/IO push

• See Cardiac Arrest



Title/2. Oxygen?
Text/
Indications: hypoxemia

• O2 10 L/min via mask



Title/3. Adrenalin?
Text/
Indication: decreased level of consciousness + hypotension (pre-arrest?)

• Adrenalin 1 mg/ml 0.5 mg (10 µg/kg) IM; repeat PRN

If no response:
• Fetch Adrenalin 0.1 mg/ml
• Fetch a 10 ml syringe
• Dra up 1 ml Adrenalin (100 µg)
• Dilute with 9 ml NaCl (yields a 10 µg/ml solution)
• Give 1 ml (10 µg) IV push for both adults and children; repeat PRN



Title/4. External Pacing?
Text/
Indication: decreased level of consciousness + hypotension + not responding to adrenalin

• Start with 100 mA and frequency of 60/min

• Feel for femoral pulse to ensure "capture",  titrate energy

• Ketamin 10 mg/ml 1 mg/kg IV over 1 min for sedation/analgesia



Title/5. Atropine?
Text/
Indication: symptomatic (e.g. presyncope, dyspnea)

• Atropine 0.5 mg/ml 0.5 mg (20 µg/kg) IV (can repeat up to 3 mg in adults)



Title/6. Isoprenalin?
Text/
Indication: symptomatic + not responding to atropine

• Isoprenaline 0.2 mg/ml 2 ml in 48 ml saline = 8 µg/ml

• Infusion rate 0.05 µg/kg/min	



Title/7. Specific Causes?
Text/
• Acquire 12-lead EKG, bedside blood tests, history

• Myocardial ischemia: Theophylline 100-200 mg by slow IV infusion may be considered in the setting of inferior myocardial infarction, cardiac transplant or spinal cord injury

• Hyperkalemia: Calcium Gluconate 100 mg/ml 30 ml (0.3 ml/kg) IV over 5 min + see Hyperkalemia

• Hypoglycemia: Glucose 300 mg/ml 30 ml (Glucose 100 mg/ml 2 ml/kg) IV

• Calcium Channel/Beta Blocker poisoning: Calcium Gluconate 100 mg/ml 30 ml IV over 5 min + see Calcium Channel/Beta Blocker poisoning?

• Digoxin Poisoning: Digoxin-specific antibody (Fab) fragments
