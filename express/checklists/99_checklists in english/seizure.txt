Title/1. Cardiac Arrest / Shock?
Text/
• Convulsions can result from hypoxia / shock / cardiac arrest



Title/2. Cervical Spine Stabilization?
Text/
Indication: head trauma prior to or upon seizure onset



Title/3. Airway Opening Maneuvers?
Text/
Indication: upper airway obstruction

• Jaw-thrust
AND/OR
• Nasopharyngeal airway



Title/4. Vomiting?
Text/
• Lateral decubitus position



Title/5. Oxygen
Text/
• Oxygen ≥ 10 L/min via mask with reservoir



Title/6. Ventilation?
Text/
Indication: low respiratory rate / low tidal volumes

• Bag-valve-mask 2-person technique



Title/7. Benzodiazepine?
Text/
Indication: continuous or intermittent seizures > 5 min

If IV access:
• Diazepam 5 mg/ml 2 ml IV (0.2 mg/kg)

If no IV access:
• Midazolam 5 mg/ml 2 ml IM (0.2 mg/kg)
OR
• Midazolam 5 mg/ml 1 ml intranasal in each nostril (0.2 mg/kg total)

• Can repeat Benzo dose once


Title/8. Crystalloid?
Text/
Indication: SBP < 120 mm Hg (cerebral blood flow is pressure-dependent during seizures)

• Crystalloid 500 ml IV bolus


Title/9. Reversible Causes?
Text/

• Hypoglycemia: Glucose 300 mg/ml 20 ml IV (glucose 100 mg/ml 2 ml/kg)

• Thiamine 500 mg IV if chronic malnutrition

• Hyponatremia: Addex-Na 4 mmol/ml 20 ml added to 0.9% NaCl 250 ml. Give 270 ml IV over 15 minutes (4 ml/kg for children)

• Hypocalcemia: Calcium Gluconate 100 mg/ml 10 ml IV over 5 min

• Meningitis: Ceftriaxone 3 g IV + Ampicillin 3 g IV or equivalent

• Encephalitis: Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV

• Na channel poisoning: NaHCO3 50 mg/ml 200 ml IV push (3 ml/kg)

• Isoniazid poisoning: Pyridoxine 5 g IV over 10 min

• Eclampsia: Addex®-Magnesium 1 mmol/ml 20 ml IV over 5 min (5 g) OR Magnesium sulphate 500 mg/ml 10 ml IM in each thigh (10 g total)



Title/10. Keppra = Levetiracetam?
Text/
Indication: continuous or intermittent seizures lasting > 5 min regardless of response to benzo

• Levetiracetam (Keppra®) 60 mg/kg (max 4.5 g) IV over 10 min


Title/11. Propofol?
Text/
Indication: continuous seizures

• Propofol 2 mg/kg IV bolus



Title/12. Deep Sedation and Endotracheal Intubation?
Text/
Indication: continued seizure despite Benzo + Keppra

Endotracheal intubation with following induction:
• Propofol 2-5 mg/kg IV	over 5 min
OR
• Tiopental (Pentothal®) 2-7 mg/kg IV max 50 mg/min
OR
• Midazolam (Dormicum®)	0.2 mg/kg IV



Title/13. Further Management
Text/
• Blood tests: Na, Ca, Mg, glucose, CBC, anticonvulsant levels

• Head CT if first seizure OR head trauma

• EEG if non-convulsive status OR received neuromuscular blocking agent

• Hyperthermia should be treated (increases brain metabolism)
